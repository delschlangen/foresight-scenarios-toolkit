# Contingency Planning: Scenario Response Playbooks

Pre-developed responses enable rapid action when scenarios shift. This document provides templates for building scenario-specific contingency plans.

---

## Why Contingency Planning Matters

When a critical indicator triggers or scenario probability shifts dramatically, you don't have time to develop strategy from scratch. Pre-built playbooks enable:

- **Faster response** â€” Decisions are pre-made, just execute
- **Better quality** â€” Plans developed calmly, not in crisis
- **Organizational alignment** â€” Everyone knows the plan
- **Reduced panic** â€” The future feels less uncertain when you're prepared

## Worked Example: Patchwork Progress Playbook

### Playbook: Patchwork Progress

**Last Updated:** 2024-12
**Owner:** VP Strategy
**Review Frequency:** Quarterly

---

#### Trigger Conditions

| Trigger Type | Condition | Verification |
|--------------|-----------|--------------|

**Activation Authority:** Chief Strategy Officer

#### Phase 1: Immediate Actions (0-30 days)

| Priority | Action | Owner | Dependencies | Complete By |
|----------|--------|-------|--------------|-------------|
| 1 | Brief executive team on scenario implications | VP Strategy | None | Day 1 |

| 3 | Map product portfolio to EU risk categories | Product | Legal input | Day 14 |
| 4 | Identify immediate compliance gaps | Compliance | Audit complete | Day 21 |
| 5 | Develop 90-day compliance acceleration plan | Legal + Product | Gap analysis | Day 30 |

- All-hands briefing on regulatory landscape: Week 2
- Customer communication on compliance status: Week 4

## Playbook Activation Checklist

When activating a scenario playbook:

- [ ] Verify trigger conditions are met (not false positive)
- [ ] Confirm with secondary data source if possible
- [ ] Notify activation authority for approval
- [ ] Record activation decision and rationale
- [ ] Communicate activation to relevant stakeholders
- [ ] Assign action owners with deadlines
- [ ] Schedule first checkpoint review (Day 7)
- [ ] Update scenario probabilities in main tracking

---

## Playbook Maintenance

### Quarterly Review

Every quarter, review each playbook for:

- [ ] Are trigger conditions still relevant?
- [ ] Have any triggers been partially met?
- [ ] Are immediate actions still executable?
- [ ] Are resource assumptions still valid?
- [ ] Are dependencies still available?
- [ ] Have scenario dynamics changed?

### After Activation

If a playbook is activated (even partially):

- [ ] Document what worked and what didn't
- [ ] Update playbook based on lessons learned
- [ ] Assess whether activation was timely
- [ ] Identify early warning improvements
- [ ] Share learnings across organization
